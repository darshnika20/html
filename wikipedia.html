Overview

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. It is the fifth-largest satellite in the Solar System and the largest relative to the size of its planet.

Features

The Moon has a differentiated structure: crust, mantle, and core. Its surface is covered in regolith and features impact craters, volcanic plains, and lunar highlands.

Exploration

Human exploration began in 1959 with the Soviet Luna program. NASA's Apollo program successfully landed astronauts on the Moon between 1969 and 1972.
